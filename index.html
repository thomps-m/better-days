Recognise

These are among the most common mental health challenges - recognising them is the first step. Hover over each card to see signs to look for.

〰 Anxiety

A persistent sense of worry or unease that feels hard to switch off, even when there's no immediate threat.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Constant or intrusive worrying
  • Restlessness and difficulty concentrating
  • Racing heart, shallow breathing, muscle tension
  • Avoiding situations that trigger worry
  • Difficulty sleeping due to overthinking

◌ Depression

More than sadness - a persistent heaviness that affects motivation, relationships, and your sense of self.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Low mood lasting more than two weeks
  • Loss of interest in activities you used to enjoy
  • Changes in appetite, sleep, or energy
  • Feelings of hopelessness, guilt, or worthlessness
  • Difficulty making decisions or concentrating

⬡ Burnout

The result of long-term, unresolved stress — often from work or caring for others — leaving you emotionally and physically depleted.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Deep emotional exhaustion
  • Feeling detached or cynical about your work or role
  • Reduced sense of accomplishment
  • Unable to switch off or fully rest
  • Recurring illness or persistent headaches

○ Loneliness

The gap between the connection you have and the connection you need - felt in a crowd as much as alone.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Feeling misunderstood even around others
  • Withdrawing from social situations
  • Excessive time online as a substitute for connection
  • Difficulty forming or sustaining close relationships

≈ Stress

The body's natural pressure response. Short-term stress is normal — but chronic stress takes a toll on health and wellbeing.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Irritability, mood swings, or outbursts
  • Trouble falling or staying asleep
  • Feeling overwhelmed or out of control
  • Relying on alcohol, screens, or food to cope
  • Tension headaches or digestive issues

◇ Low self-esteem

A persistent negative view of yourself that shapes how you feel about your worth, abilities, and place in the world.
Hover to see signs →

Signs to look for

  • Harsh and constant self-criticism
  • Difficulty accepting praise or compliments
  • Comparing yourself negatively to others
  • People-pleasing or avoiding conflict at all costs
  • Strong fear of failure or rejection
If you recognise these patterns in yourself or someone you care about, speaking with your GP or a mental health professional is always a good first step.

Finding Support

If you're going through a difficult time, talking to someone can help more than you might expect. These services are free, confidential, and available right now.

Reaching out is not a sign of weakness - it takes courage to admit that you might need help. You don't need to be in crisis to call. You just need to be struggling.
